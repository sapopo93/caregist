Data Completeness Tiers — Explanation
=====================================

CareGist assigns a "data completeness" tier to each provider listing.
This measures how much information is available in our records — it is
NOT a quality rating or inspection outcome.

  COMPLETE (score 85-100): All key fields populated — name, address,
    phone, website, coordinates, CQC rating, service types, specialisms,
    inspection dates, and more.

  GOOD (score 60-84): Most key fields populated. Some secondary fields
    like website, specialisms, or bed count may be missing.

  PARTIAL (score 40-59): Core fields present (name, postcode, rating)
    but several important fields are missing.

  SPARSE (score below 40): Minimal data available. Only basic
    identification fields are populated.

These tiers are completely separate from CQC inspection ratings
(Outstanding, Good, Requires Improvement, Inadequate). A provider with
a "SPARSE" data completeness tier may still have an "Outstanding" CQC
rating — it simply means we have limited data about them in our records.

For official CQC inspection ratings, always check:
https://www.cqc.org.uk
